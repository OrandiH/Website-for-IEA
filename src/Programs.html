J1 Summer Work & Travel

Step 1: Registration

Requirements

  1. Must be 18—29 years old.

  2. Must pay a non–refundable registration fee of JMD $2500.

  3. Must be a full-time student at a tertiary institution.

  4. Must have successfully completed at least one semester with at 2.0 GPA.

  5. 2 passport size photos (not certified).

  6. 1 copy of passport information page.

  7. 1 copy of resume.

  8. Copy of school ID.

  9. Medical report.

  10. Police record.

  11. Certified (signed & stamped) student status letter from institution and transcript.

  12. Completed application form and self introductory video.


A non-refundable deposit of USD $200 must be paid at the time of registration to secure your application.


Registration ends November 30th NO EXCEPTION.


Step 2: Application Process

  1. Submit transcript & deposit program fee of US $500 by November 30th.

    2. All fees must be paid by January 30th.


  2. Total Program Fee Cost: New/Returning Students:

    • $1400 USD for Full Placement

    • $1480 USD for Skype/Job fair

    • Make all payments to NCB A/C #304855711 Half Way Tree branch.


Please note

All lodgment slips must be kept and presented to our office for payment confirmation. NB. Payment can be made in installments. Jobs are not guaranteed until full payment of fee. Program fee is to be paid in US dollars ONLY and DOES NOT include embassy appointment fees. Deadline for all program fee is January 30th After full program fee payment, you will be updated on job placement, orientation and embassy.


Program fee covers:

  • Pre-screening & application processing.
  • Job placement and verification.
  • DS-2019 Form (Work Permit).
  • Medical/health insurance coverage(only lasts for program duration).
  • Embassy interview pre preparation.
  • In country advice assistance.

ADDITIONAL FEES THAT WILL APPLY

  • Orientation pre-departure & post arrival.
  • Visa appointment.

Cancellation Policy:

If a student cancels before going to the embassy after being placed , they will lose 25% of program fee and will be refunded no later than six weeks. If a student is unsuccessful at the embassy and is denied a J1 visa, that student will lose 25% and will be refunded no later than six weeks after visa denial once DS form is submitted. If students are not placed by May 15 , refunds will become available at the end of May.
